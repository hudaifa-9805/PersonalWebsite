About Me

The Story Behind
the Resume

I'm a Finance student with a focus in FinTech at UT Dallas, with real experience building and shipping products across AI startups and Big 4 consulting.

At EY, I worked directly on client products, owning documentation and driving changes from a security and risk standpoint, translating complex compliance requirements into clear, actionable specs that engineers and stakeholders could actually move on. At Headstarter AI, I led a team through a full product build, taking an LLM-powered Q&A bot from problem definition to launch, writing PRDs, setting success metrics, and keeping everyone aligned from discovery through delivery.

I'm passionate about FinTech because financial products have some of the highest potential to create real user impact. What draws me to PM specifically is the natural pull I feel toward leading teams and owning outcomes. I can read code and get into implementation details with engineers, then turn around and translate that into risk, business, or user impact terms for the stakeholders who need to make decisions. Being the connective tissue between those worlds is where I do my best work.

What makes my background a little different is that I came into college as a CS major with over 10 years of coding experience, but most of that code came from pure curiosity rather than assignments. When school started dictating what I built and why, it stopped feeling like mine. I made a deliberate move into Finance with a FinTech focus because I wanted to lead, think from a business perspective, and build things driven by real problems rather than coursework. That shift gave me the best of both sides — the technical fluency to work alongside engineers and the business grounding to make sure what gets built actually matters.
